How women rights are violated in the world?

Referring to the Amnesty International: Women rights are human rights, which are all entitled to the same rights of humans. These include the right to live free from violence and discrimination and enjoy the highest attainable standard of physical and mental health, like also to be educated, own a property have rights to vote and to earn an equal wage.

But across the entire globe there are still many women and girls that facing discrimination on the daily basis of sex and gender. Gender inequality underpins many problems, which disproportionately affect women and girls, such as domestic and sexual violence, lower pay, lack of access to education, and inadequate healthcare.

Referring to the CANWACH, There are 6 main ways of women human rights which are violated around the world every day through gender-based violence:
    Intimate Partner Violence



    The consequences for physical and mental health are serious as one in three women worldwide has experienced physical or sexual abuse. It is estimated that 70% of all female murders are committed by male intimate partners. Women who experience partner violence are 1.5 times more likely to contract a range of sexually transmitted diseases. Women exposed to violence are twice as likely to be depressed.
    Female Genital Mutilation/Cutting



    Today, around the world, 200 million women live with the dissection other words mutilation of the female genitalia, a practice, that involves multiple health risks including hemorrhage, infertility, continuous severe pelvic pain, complications in childbirth, HIV, psychological trauma and death.
    Child Marriage



    Child marriage is one of the most harmful forms of physical and mental abuse for young girls. The number of women and girls alive today who married before their 18th birthday is estimated at 650 million. Girls who marry in childhood are more likely to experience violence by their partner, than girls of the same age who marry later. (9 of 10 countries with the highest child marriage rates are in fragile states).
    Humanitarian Crises



    Humanitarian crises, including conflicts - wars and post-conflict situations like tsunami or tornadoes, increase women's vulnerability to violence. In some depressed countries, 70 percent of women experience gender-based violence. More than half of all maternal, infant and child deaths occur in humane conditions. According to recent statistics, Gender equality is not prioritized in humanitarian aid. There is only 5% of foreign aid to fragile states is directed towards gender equality.

The Report from 2016 about Human Trafficking shows, that 71% of all trafficking victims worldwide are women and girls and 75% of trafficked women and girls are exploited for sexual slavery.
    Economic Inequalities



    Violence against women and girls has a negative impact on the entire national economy and the ability of women to live a full civic life. Violence seriously affects women's right to live a normal live. It is estimated, that the earnings of women exposed to severe partner violence in permanent employment are 60% lower than those of women who do not. It also has serious economic impact to whole society. The cost of violence against women was estimated by last years. Taking a comprehensive approach, including healthcare costs, recently researchers calculated, that violence against women costs the United States more than $ 500 billion a year.
